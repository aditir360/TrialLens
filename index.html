Frequently Asked Questions

Cancer clinical trials are research studies that test new ways to prevent, detect, treat, or manage cancer. They give patients access to promising new treatments before they are widely available, while helping doctors learn which approaches work best.
Each trial has specific eligibility criteria including cancer type, stage, previous treatments, age, and overall health. Our Trial Finder tool helps match you with relevant trials based on your specific situation.
Clinical trials follow strict safety protocols and are closely monitored by medical experts and regulatory agencies. All potential risks and benefits are clearly explained before you decide to participate.
Most clinical trials provide the experimental treatment at no cost to participants. Some trials may also cover additional medical costs, travel expenses, or provide compensation for time and participation.
What is the issue we are trying to solve?

Every year, thousands of cancer patients miss out on potentially life-saving clinical trials — not because they aren't eligible, but because the information is buried in medical jargon, scattered across databases, and hard to navigate. Less than 5% of adult cancer patients enroll in trials, even though trials are critical for advancing treatments and improving survival rates. By making trial information clear and accessible, we’re helping more patients find and join research that could save lives. Every match and every story adds to the data we can share with policymakers, researchers, and healthcare leaders to break down barriers to participation. Our mission goes beyond guidance — it’s about shaping a future where clinical trial access is equitable, transparent, and a standard part of cancer care.
